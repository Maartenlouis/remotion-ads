# Natural Transitions

Transitional phrases for ad scripts and copy. Good transitions improve flow and help avoid AI-sounding content.

---

## For Video Ad Scripts

### Scene Transitions
- "Here's the thing:"
- "But what most people don't know..."
- "So what can you actually do?"
- "The good news?"
- "That's where we come in."

### Problem to Solution
- "But it doesn't have to be this way."
- "There's a better approach."
- "Here's what you can do instead:"
- "You have options."

### Solution to CTA
- "Ready to take the next step?"
- "Want to find out where you stand?"
- "Here's how to get started:"
- "Your next step:"

---

## For Carousel Slides

### Slide-to-Slide Flow
- "Here's why this matters..."
- "But that's not all."
- "The biggest mistake?"
- "What you should do instead:"
- "The key takeaway:"

### Final Slide
- "Don't wait until it's too late."
- "Your rights have deadlines."
- "Get clarity today."
- "Questions? Reach out."

---

## For Social Captions

### Opening Lines
- "PSA for anyone who just bought a house:"
- "True story:"
- "This happens more often than you'd think:"
- "Quick question:"

### Mid-Caption
- "Here's the key takeaway:"
- "What does this mean for you?"
- "The short version:"
- "In plain language:"

### Closing / CTA
- "Save this for later."
- "Share with someone who needs this."
- "DM us your questions."
- "Link in Bio for the full guide."

## Phrases to Avoid (AI Tells)

These signal AI-generated content:

| Avoid | Use Instead |
|-------|-------------|
| "That being said,..." | "But..." / "However,..." |
| "It's worth noting that..." | "Important:" / "Key point:" |
| "At its core,..." | "Basically:" / "The main idea:" |
| "In today's digital landscape,..." | (Just skip it) |
| "When it comes to the realm of..." | "When it comes to..." |
| "This begs the question..." | "This raises a question:" |
| "Let's delve into..." | "Let's look at..." |
| "Moreover,..." | "Also,..." / "Plus,..." |
| "Furthermore,..." | "And,..." / "On top of that,..." |
| "In conclusion,..." | "The bottom line:" / "To sum up:" |

---

## Hedging Language

For claims that need qualification:

- "may," "might," "could"
- "often," "usually," "typically"
- "in most cases"
- "evidence suggests"
- "many experts recommend"

Use hedging for legal/medical/financial claims. Be direct for everything else.
